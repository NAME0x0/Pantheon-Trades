# Athens Mapping

The entire system is named using ancient Athenian democracy metaphors. This document maps each component to its historical analogue.

|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|

| Pythia | Πυθία | Oracle of Delphi — source of prophetic data |

## System Topology as a City

Think of the system as an Athenian city-state:

```
Olympus (Mount) ── supreme governance, looking down on all

  Agora (Marketplace) ── signals flow, information traded
       │
  Pnyx (Assembly Hill) ── voting and deliberation (Boule)
       │
  Areopagus (Sacred Rock) ── veto court, final review
       │
  Strategos (Field Command) ── execution
       │
  Parthenon (Temple Archive) ── permanent record
       │
  Elysium / Underworld ── afterlife for strategies
```

## Why Athens?

Prediction markets are fundamentally about collective intelligence — aggregating dispersed information into probabilities. Democratic Athens was the original collective intelligence system: a city that made decisions by deliberation, debate, and structured voting rather than royal decree.

The naming is intentional: every component is designed to reflect the strengths of Athenian democracy (diverse voices, adversarial debate, checks and balances) while avoiding its weaknesses (mob rule, unchecked demagoguery — hence Zeus and Solon's vetoes).
